Explain the risks

You must get the participant’s permission to carry out a mammogram on breasts with implants.

There is no medical reason for people with breast implants to avoid the procedure. The benefits of early cancer detection outweigh the minor increase in risk.

Explain to them that:

  • very rarely, implants can rupture due to pressure during imaging
  • a special imaging technique will be used to reduce the likelihood of complications
  • implants make it harder to spot signs of cancer as can they obscure breast tissue
  • extra images may be needed to scan enough breast tissue, slightly increasing radiation exposure

If the participant notices any problems with their implants after the appointment, they should contact their GP.

Once you’ve explained the process and the risks, ask them to read and complete your paper consent form.

Difficulties interpreting images

Mammograms with breast implants can be less effective at detecting breast cancer because the implants can obscure some breast tissue.
